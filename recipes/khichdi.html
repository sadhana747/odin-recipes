Spicy Vegetable Khichdi Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup basmati rice
  • 1/2 cup split yellow moong dal (lentils)
  • 1 tablespoon ghee or oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, beans, etc.), chopped
  • 1 green chili, finely chopped (adjust to taste)
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 4 cups water (adjust as needed)
  • Fresh cilantro leaves, chopped, for garnish
  • Lemon wedges for serving

Instructions:

  1. Wash the rice and moong dal together until the water runs clear. Soak them in water for about 30 minutes, then drain.
  2. In a pressure cooker or a large pot, heat ghee or oil over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  3. Add the finely chopped onion and sauté until it becomes translucent.
  4. Stir in the ginger-garlic paste and chopped green chili. Sauté for a minute until the raw smell disappears.
  5. Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn soft and the oil starts to separate.
  6. Add the mixed vegetables, turmeric powder, red chili powder, cumin powder, garam masala, and salt. Mix well and cook for a few minutes.
  7. Add the soaked and drained rice and lentils. Mix everything together to coat the rice and lentils with the spices.
  8. Pour in the water and give it a good stir.
  9. If using a pressure cooker, close the lid and cook for 3-4 whistles. If using a pot, cover it and cook on medium-low heat until the rice and lentils are cooked and the khichdi reaches the desired consistency. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking.
  10. Once cooked, let the pressure release naturally if using a pressure cooker.
  11. Garnish the spicy khichdi with chopped cilantro leaves.
  12. Serve the khichdi hot with a squeeze of lemon juice on top.
